DCP Test Locations

Where to put your DCP readings on a landing zone — full survey vs. hasty survey. Pattern per the SAAZA course (Unit 3).
Running each test
  • Two people. An operator holds the DCP vertical and works the slide hammer; a recorder reads and logs the depths.
  • Seat the cone. Tap until the base of the cone is flush with the surface, then take a baseline reading.
  • Each blow. Raise the hammer to the bottom of the handle (don't lift the handle itself), then release and let it drop.
  • Depth. Test to the full depth of the rod — 36 in rods are adequate for semi-prepared surfaces.
  • Stay plumb. If the handle leans more than 6 in off vertical, stop and start a fresh test right next to it. Never force the handle down. (Same response if you hit refusal and the cone stops advancing: relocate and retry.)
  • Record to the nearest 0.2 in (5 mm), on the DCP Data Sheet, for every test.
  • Range. The DCP reads from CBR <1 up to >100.
Test pattern & priority order: SAAZA Unit 3 (consistent with ETL 97-9, Fig C.2). Procedure: ETL 97-9 / TSPWG 3-260-03.02-19 — both Approved for Public Release, Distribution Unlimited. Diagram drawn original; oriented vertically for phone screens.
